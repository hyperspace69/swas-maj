About

I am Swastik Majumder, originally from Kolkata, West Bengal, India, and currently a graduate student in the Department of Physics at The Ohio State University, where I am pursuing a PhD in Physics. My research interests lie in problems where complex collective behavior emerges from the interplay of interactions, geometry, and thermal effects. My previous work has focused on stochastic transport, interacting particle systems, and disordered geometries. At present, I am interested in the properties of hot and dense quark matter, with particular emphasis on perturbative quantum chromodynamics (QCD).

Beyond my research, I maintain a broad intellectual interest in fiction, non-fiction, philosophy, history, and political history. I also write poetry, keep notes on books I read, enjoy teaching physics, and follow cricket closely.

You may contact me at majumderswastik09@gmail.com.
