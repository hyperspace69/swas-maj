About

I am Swastik Majumder from Kolkata, West Bengal, India. I am currently a graduate student at the Ohio State University, where I am pursuing a PhD in Physics. I am drawn to problems where complex behavior emerges from interplay between interactions and thermal effects. My past work has focused on stochastic transport, interacting particle systems, and disordered geometries. Currently, I am interested in understanding the behavior of hot and dense quark matter using perturbative quantum choromodynamics (QCD).

Beyond physics, I am an avid reader of fiction, non-fiction, philosophy, history, and especially the history of politics. I also write poetry, blog about books I have been reading, enjoy teaching physics, and closely follow cricket.

You can contact me at majumderswastik09@gmail.com.

Research papers

Stochastic systems with Bose-Hubbard interactions: Effects of bias on particles on a random comb

Swastik Majumder, Mustansir Barma

Physical Review E, accepted article (2026)

Studies stochastic transport of interacting particles on a disordered random-comb network, emphasizing how bias and interactions shape steady-state behavior.

Stochastic systems with Bose-Hubbard interactions: Effects of bias on particles on a one-dimensional lattice

Swastik Majumder, Mustansir Barma

Physical Review E, accepted article (2026)

Explores the interplay between drive, interactions, and geometry in stochastic lattice systems on a one-dimensional lattice.

View paper

Three cases of complex eigenvalue/vector distributions of symmetric order-three random tensors

Swastik Majumder, Naoki Sasakura

Progress of Theoretical and Experimental Physics (2024)

We computed complex eigenvalue and eigenvector distributions for symmetric order-three random tensors using zero-dimensional quantum field theory techniques. These distributions find application in quantum gravity, spin glass physics and quantum information theory.
