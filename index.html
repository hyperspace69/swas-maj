About

I am a physics PhD student at The Ohio State University working on statistical physics, field theory, and related many-body problems.

I am Swastik Majumder from Kolkata, West Bengal, India. I am drawn to problems where complex behavior emerges from interactions, thermal effects, bias, disorder, and collective dynamics. My work has focused on stochastic transport, interacting particle systems, and disordered geometries, and I am currently also interested in quark matter at high temperature and density, especially in relation to the equation of state of neutron stars.

Beyond physics, I am an avid reader of non-fiction, philosophy, history, and especially the history of politics. I also write poetry, blog about books I have been reading, enjoy teaching physics, and closely follow cricket.

You can contact me at majumder.31@osu.edu.

Research summary

What I work on

My research lies broadly in theoretical physics. I like problems in which rich collective behavior appears because of interactions, fluctuations, thermal effects, constraints, and geometry. These themes show up across my work in statistical physics, field theory, stochastic dynamics, and many-body systems, and they also motivate my current interest in quark matter at high temperature and density and in the equation of state relevant for neutron stars.
